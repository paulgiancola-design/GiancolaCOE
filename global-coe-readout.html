Executive Summary

Across five leadership sessions — including the June 3–4 TMT CBS Boston Offsite — a consistent and expanding set of responsibilities surfaced that the Global Center of Excellence must own. The COE is explicitly described as the "connective tissue" and innovation multiplier for the field: innovating globally, then federating wins back to each OU. The hard deadline to demonstrate referenceable proof points is La Lima (mid-August 2026).

The Boston Offsite surfaced four new critical ownership areas beyond prior meetings: SAGE Engagement Motion Enablement (Seed–Attach–Grow–Expand commercial shift), Sales Approval Workflow Rationalization (documented "infinite loop" of approval gates), Value Capture & "90-Day Look Back" Methodology, and AI Tool Visibility & Access Governance (standardize visibility first, then delegate official standardization). The team also formalized COE Immediate Priorities: scoping process assessment, tool evaluation, feedback loops, and OU alignment — plus a cross-OU AI Champions / Innovation Scoring Group to evaluate field hackathon submissions and federate winners globally.

The heaviest concentration of COE ownership sits at the intersection of Pre-Sales solutioning (qualifying, scoping, and packaging deals in the Quantum Leap model) and Delivery governance (standardizing AI-native tools, methodology, and measurement). Eight items are rated Critical: Day One Readiness, Quantum Leap Methodology, Scoping Standardization, Agentic CoE Offering, Global Tool Inventory, Productivity Reporting, SAGE Enablement, and Sales Approval Rationalization.
