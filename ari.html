📌 What is ARI?

The Attrition Reduction Investment (ARI) program funds Salesforce Professional Services delivery at no cost to the customer, in exchange for a renewal commitment. It is a strategic lever used on Red Accounts where PS work is the critical enabler of preventing contract attrition.

The pitch to customers: "Salesforce will send in-house experts to implement at zero cost — all we need is your renewal commitment."

ARI is distinct from a standard Services Work Exception (SWE). It is specifically scoped to accounts where customer attrition is at risk and where ProServ engagement — adoption, implementation, enablement — directly addresses the root cause of that risk.

⚠️ Recent Change — ROI Threshold Raised Leadership raised the minimum ROI requirement from 1x to 2x in FY27 to prioritize high-impact requests and ensure the best possible return on investments. All current and future submissions must meet this bar.

✅ Eligibility Criteria

Required (Must Meet All)

  • $500K+ forecasted attrition on a renewal opportunity
  • Renewal in FY27 or FY28
  • 2x ROI or higher on attrition mitigation (e.g., $1M investment to save $2M+)
  • Account is a designated Red Account
  • Attrition risk is mitigable by Salesforce Professional Services
  • Aligned with Sales Account Team & Sales OU Leadership

Preferred (Increases Approval Likelihood)

  • Multi-year renewal (3+ years preferred)
  • Customer intent to renew the at-risk footprint
  • Full save not required — partial saves qualify if AOV saved exceeds $500K and hits 2x ROI
  • Customer has confirmed executive sponsorship for the work
  • Early renewal to pull forward ACV
Note on 1-Year Renewals: ARI can be approved even if the customer renews for just 1 year — confirmed by leadership. Multi-year is preferred but not a hard block.

🗺️ Process Map

1

Verify Eligibility & Align Team

Confirm the account meets all required criteria: Red Account status, FY renewal timing, $500K+ attrition risk, and that ProServ can address the root cause.

  • Align with Account SE and CSG Account Partner to verbally scope business use-case
  • Agree on the appropriate investment amount (ROM) needed for the SOW and PS implementation
  • AP opens an Opportunity in Org62 for the ARI — even at Stage 1 (required)
2

Complete ARI Request Form & Exec Summary

Fill out the Basecamp ARI Request Form with account details, attrition risk amount, root cause (Adoption/Performance/Implementation), and specific challenges.

  • A Slack thread is created in #sales-sci-requests within ~1 business day of submission
  • Create the Exec Summary slide using the approved template (Slide 17 of the enablement deck)
  • Link the Exec Summary in the Basecamp ticket and the #sales-sci-requests Slack thread
  • Update the Red Account ECOMM Headline to include #ARI
  • Ensure the Forecasted Attrition field on the Red Account record reflects the full amount at risk
3

Submit for Approval Review

Submission flows automatically via Basecamp into the ARI Committee's Slack approval workflow. The committee meets weekly to review submissions.

  • The four-level approval chain progresses sequentially (see Approval Chain section)
  • Scoping conversations with customers may begin after L3 (PS Geo Lead) approval
  • Do not commit an investment amount to the customer until full L4 approval
  • Expedited/async review is available for time-sensitive cases with leadership support
  • Full approval timeline: 4–6 weeks minimum
4

Secure Customer Commitment & Renewal

Once approval is in progress or received, secure a customer commitment to renew contingent on the ARI investment. This can be a verbal, email, or SOW-level commitment.

  • ARI funding is released upon contract signature (early renewal is acceptable and preferred)
  • Ask the critical question directly: "If we do this work, will it prevent the attrition?"
  • Ideal scenario: ARI drives an early renewal, pulling forward ARR
5

ProServ Engagement Begins

Once the ARI is approved and the renewal/commitment is in place, Professional Services resources are assigned and delivery begins.

  • PS team delivers hands-on implementation, user training, adoption enablement, and knowledge transfer
  • ARI Program Manager (by Geo) oversees delivery health via weekly health check framework
  • Delivered SWE Amount is tracked against the approved investment to forecast budget consumption
  • Scope must remain tied to the original approved use case — scope drift is actively monitored

🏛️ Approval Chain

All four levels must be completed sequentially. Each level cannot proceed until the prior one is fully approved.

Level 1

Business Alignment

Criteria validation — Exec summary reviewed for eligibility, ROI, and completeness.

Level 2

OU Leader Approval

Feasibility check: PS budget & capacity. Includes OU GM, PS Geo Lead, and any OU-specific approvers.

Level 3

Geo Approval

Geographic leadership approval (e.g., Christina Meitus for AMER). Scoping conversations may begin at this stage.

Level 4

Global / Final Approval

Executive-level final approvals — includes Lori Steele, Jim Roth, and Miguel. Investment cannot be communicated to customer until this level is complete.

⚠️ Timing Conflict Risk: The 4–6 week minimum approval window frequently conflicts with urgent renewal or SIC dates. Teams should initiate ARI submissions as early as possible — ideally 8–10 weeks before the renewal date.

🔍 Identified Gaps

The following gaps were identified through Slack conversations across Red Account channels, the #ari-support-channel, DMs, and the ARI governance workstream. Severity ratings reflect frequency and business impact.

1

No Direct Customer Validation of Proposed Work HIGH

The single biggest gap identified: teams are scoping and submitting ARIs without directly asking the customer "If we do this work, will it prevent the attrition?" This results in customers receiving work that doesn't address their real issues, leading to non-renewals post-delivery. Leadership (Lori Steele) has noted this as a critical problem with scoping quality.

2

Loose & High-Level Scoping HIGH

The submission process remains high-level, and solutions often reflect "what Salesforce thinks solves the problem" rather than what the customer actually needs. The Exec Summary template provides structure but doesn't enforce customer-validated scope. Scope quality is inconsistent across geos and account teams.

3

Customer Commitment Chicken-and-Egg HIGH

The process requires documented customer intent to renew before submission, but teams cannot guarantee the ARI investment to the customer until L4 approval is received. This creates a deadlock — account teams face pushback from customers who won't commit without knowing the investment is confirmed. No clear resolution guidance exists in official documentation.

4

Notable Gap Between ARI Approval and SOW Signing HIGH

Even after L4 approval, there is a significant and unaddressed gap before an SOW is executed. No structured handoff or SLA exists between approval and SOW signature, creating delays that compress the time available for actual delivery before the renewal date.

5

Scope Drift After Approval MEDIUM

Multiple instances observed (particularly in CBS) where the original need that drove ARI approval is no longer valid, but teams attempt to repurpose the approved funds for different work. APs now being compensated on ARI closures creates incentive to close ARIs at all cost, increasing scope drift risk.

6

License & Success Team Education Gap MEDIUM

License and Customer Success teams consistently lack a clear understanding of the ARI program — what it is, who qualifies, and how it works. Teams regularly require re-education on program basics, slowing down coordination and creating submission errors. ARI submissions can only be done by Success resources (CSM or CSAL), yet many Success reps don't fully understand the process.

7

No Standardized Guidance on Customer Commitment Format MEDIUM

Multiple teams across geos (JPKT, EMEA, AMER) have asked for specific guidance on how to capture customer commitment — format, legal guardrails against "side letter" issues, and specific language to use. Current documentation says "email, SOW, or verbal commitment" but provides no standard template or legal review guidance.

8

ARI Offered to Customer Before Approvals Complete MEDIUM

Multiple instances documented where ARI investments were communicated to customers before final L4 approval, creating commitment risk and approval complications. Current guardrails are not consistently enforced across the field.

9

Field Permissions & Submission Barriers MEDIUM

Field access issues prevent smooth submission: the Forecasted Attrition field on Red Account records is not editable by all submission participants. In some cases, Sales COO approval is required before submission but teams discover this mid-process. Visibility into the approval chain within the SCI workflow is inconsistent.

10

No ARI-Specific SOW Guidance LOW

Teams preparing SOWs for ARI-funded work are unclear on which clauses, guardrails, or contract structures are required. The standard SWE $0 SOW template is being used without ARI-specific modifications, creating legal and scope accountability gaps.

11

Two-Step ARI Process Undefined LOW

When both discovery/scoping and implementation are needed (and the discovery itself is more than a standard scoping exercise), the process for submitting two sequential ARIs is unclear. Teams receive inconsistent guidance on whether to submit a single combined request or two separate ones.

💡 Recommendations

The following recommendations are prioritized by impact and feasibility. COE-owned items are marked accordingly.

🎯

Mandate a Customer Validation Conversation Before Scoping HIGH PRIORITY COE

Require account teams to document a direct customer response to: "If Salesforce invests in this work, will it prevent the attrition and secure the renewal?" This response (verbal, email, or meeting notes) should be a required field in the ARI submission form — not optional. This is the most impactful single change available.

📋

Introduce a Structured Pre-Submission Scoping Checklist HIGH PRIORITY COE

The COE should develop and require a scoping checklist before ARI submission — covering: root cause confirmed with customer, proposed scope reviewed with Account SE, customer has endorsed the work plan, and an attrition-prevention linkage has been explicitly documented. This bridges the gap between the high-level process and submission quality.

🔄

Publish a Resolution to the Customer Commitment Deadlock HIGH PRIORITY

Officially document the sequencing guidance: teams may initiate exploratory conversations about scope and intent after L3 approval, but should not commit dollar amounts until L4. Provide a standard "intent language" script that account teams can use with customers to secure conditional commitment without over-promising. This resolves the most commonly raised question in #ari-support-channel.

⏱️

Define an Approval-to-SOW SLA HIGH PRIORITY

Establish a target window (e.g., 10 business days) between L4 approval and SOW execution. Assign ownership of the SOW drafting process post-approval to the ARI Program Manager. This closes the currently unaddressed gap between approval and delivery kickoff.

📢

Launch ARI Education for License and Success Teams MEDIUM PRIORITY COE

Develop a short (10-min), evergreen ARI explainer module for CSMs, CSALs, and License teams — covering what ARI is, who qualifies, the submission process, and common mistakes. Embed it in onboarding for Success roles and distribute via the GCO weekly newsletter. The current reliance on word-of-mouth education is not scalable.

🛡️

Implement Scope Drift Controls MEDIUM PRIORITY

Require that any material change to approved ARI scope be re-submitted as a scope amendment for OU Leader review. Create a field in the ARI tracking system to flag instances where the business need at time of submission differs from the proposed SOW. The AP compensation change makes this especially urgent.

📄

Publish ARI-Specific SOW Template and Legal Guidance MEDIUM PRIORITY COE

Develop a standard ARI SOW template with required clauses (scope boundaries, renewal contingency language, deliverable ownership, no side-letter protections). Distribute via the COE and link directly from the ARI Submission Guide. Teams should not be adapting the generic SWE template for ARI use.

📝

Create a Standard Customer Commitment Template MEDIUM PRIORITY

Develop a short, legally-reviewed document (or email template) that formalizes customer intent to renew contingent on ARI investment. This removes ambiguity for account teams globally, reduces legal risk around side-letter issues, and creates a consistent paper trail for audit purposes.

🔧

Fix Field Permissions and Submission Prerequisites LOW PRIORITY

Audit and resolve: (1) Forecasted Attrition field editability on Red Account records for all submission participants, (2) Sales COO approval prerequisite — surface this requirement earlier in the submission flow, and (3) approval chain visibility in the SCI workflow so submitters can track status without relying on ad hoc Slack updates.

📐

Define the Two-Step ARI Process LOW PRIORITY COE

Publish official guidance on when a discovery-only ARI is appropriate vs. a combined discovery + implementation request. Provide a decision tree and example submissions for each path. This is a recurring question in #ari-support-channel and the current "it depends" answer creates inconsistency across geos.
